Aljazeera

Washington Post: Middle East rulers facing Corona… tightening repression instead of re-arranging priorities

The Corona crisis should have reordered the priorities of countries in the Middle East, urging them to improve governance rather than continue the identity conflicts. Instead, the crisis has provided the rulers with the opportunity to further their repression, through expanding the emergency declaration and the power of the executive in Egypt, to limiting freedom of speech in the Gulf, Morocco, and Algeria. Rather than improve their health sectors, Middle East rulers are pretending that all is well and blaming imaginary enemies according to the Washington Post article. Egypt is one example of this, as the Ministry of Health pretended that it has contained the spread of the virus and in fact has shared its plans with China and Italy, which allowed these countries to limit the spread of the virus.
