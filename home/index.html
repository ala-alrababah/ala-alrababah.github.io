Welcome! I am a Postdoctoral Fellow at the Immigration Policy Lab (IPL) at ETH Zurich. I received my Ph.D. in political science from Stanford University in 2021. My primary research is in comparative politics and international relations with a focus on authoritarian media, political violence, and migration and refugees. To study these topics, my research combines fieldwork, machine learning, and experimental methods.

Before graduate school, I was a Junior Fellow at the Carnegie Endowment for International Peace. I grew up in Amman, Jordan and graduated from Dartmouth College with a degree in Government.

My research has been published or is forthcoming in the American Political Science Review, Comparative Political Studies, Political Analysis, International Studies Quarterly, and Political Science Research and Methods. It has also been covered in several popular outlets, including Freakonomics, BBC Arabic, The Economist, The Times, The Daily Mail, Al Jazeera, Spiegel Online, Le Figaro, la Republica, and Al-Araby Al-Jadeed.

My research has been supported by Innovations for Poverty Action’s Peace and Recovery Program, Abbasi Program in Islamic Studies, Stanford Institute for Economic Policy Research (SIEPR), Stanford Center on International Conflict and Negotiation (SCICN), and Stanford Center on Philanthropy and Civil Society, among others.
